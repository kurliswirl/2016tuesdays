Biography

Naomi Goldstein is the founder, CEO, and Media Director of EnviroMagazine. She is the author of New York Times Bestseller, How To Make A Difference- Changing the World Environmentally, which sold 300 million copies in 2016. She also runs EnviroTek, a company that works for installing and building new solar panels. She has been fighting for environmental rights since she came out of the womb, marching in activist marches, and taking many environmental issues with her to the courtroom. Naomi loves to tour the world in her environmentally-friendly helicopter to solve many environmental issues globally. She lives with her three cats, the Spice Cats, and her family in Massachusetts.

Elsie Salkever is a CEO, Web Designer, and Photo Editor of EnvironMagazine. She has designed many websites globally, including the Cat Bomb website, where you can play games to try to stop cats from exploding. She has helped many, and she is an environmental activist. She has campaigned for children with better environmental futures. She lives with her family and three dogs with names that all start with M’s, as in Massachusetts. Her net worth is $600 million.

Julia Kezer is a CEO, Photographer, and De-Bugger of EnvironMagazine. She helps flick off all the viruses and adoring people who arrive at the Website and HQ. She is an activist, helping people make posters and taking photos of marches and gathering. With Elsie Salkever’s help, she launched the photography website A Life In Photos. She has gained millions of hits in the two years it has been up. She lives in Massachusetts with her family and black cat Pearl. She is also the director of The Cat Bomb, starring Reb Fezzelfoot. It's based off of Elsie's website.

Theresa Franklin is a local native who volunteered to do an article. She is part of the Section Fourtribe.
